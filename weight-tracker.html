Setup anchoring

You've two flat bands — yellow lighter, red harder. Four tricks: (1) stand on the middle for curls, raises and RDLs; (2) knot one end and shut it in a closed door (knot on the far side) for rows, chest press and Pallof; (3) wrap the ends a couple of turns round your hands to shorten the band and make it harder; (4) tie both ends together into a loop for the glute bridge. To progress without buying more: shorten your grip, go yellow → red, or use both bands at once.

Squat (chair / box) 2–3 × 12–15

No kit, knee-friendly. Stand in front of a chair, feet shoulder-width. Push your hips back and lower under control to lightly touch the seat (don't flop down), then drive through your heels to stand. Weight in your heels, knees tracking over your toes. Use a lower seat over time for more depth.

Chest press 2–3 × 12–15

No anchor needed: run the band across your upper back (under the shoulder blades) and hold an end in each hand at chest level, elbows bent. Press forward until your arms are straight, then return slowly. Wrap the ends shorter for more resistance.

Band row 2–3 × 12–15

Knot the band into a door at chest height (or sit on the floor, legs out, and loop it round your feet). Wrap an end round each hand, arms straight, step back to tension it. Pull to your ribs, squeezing your shoulder blades together, elbows skimming past your sides. Control it back out.

Glute bridge 2–3 × 12–15

Tie a band into a loop and sit it just above your knees. Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Drive through your heels and lift your hips into a straight line from shoulders to knees, pushing the knees gently outward against the loop. Squeeze the glutes at the top, lower slowly. No knee loading.

Plank 2–3 × 20–40 sec

No kit. Forearms under shoulders, body in a straight line from head to heels — backside neither sagging nor piked. Brace your stomach as if about to be poked, breathe steadily, hold. Drop to your knees if your lower back starts to dip.

Romanian deadlift (hip hinge) 2–3 × 12

Stand on the middle of the band, an end in each hand, knees softly bent but still. Push your hips back and slide your hands down your shins, back flat. Drive your hips forward to stand tall. The bend is at the hips, not the knees.

Lateral raise 2 × 12–15

Stand on the middle of the band, an end in each hand by your sides. Raise both arms out sideways to shoulder height with a slight elbow bend, then lower slowly. Lead with the elbows; don't shrug. Wrap the ends shorter if it's too easy.

Pull-apart 2 × 15

No anchor: hold the band out in front at shoulder height, hands shoulder-width, arms straight. Pull it apart by squeezing your shoulder blades together until your arms are wide, then return slowly. Hands closer together = harder. Brilliant for posture.

Press-up 2–3 × 6–12

No kit. Hands under shoulders, body in a straight line. Lower your chest under control with elbows angled back (not flared), then press up. Too hard? Do them on an incline — hands on the bed, sofa or kitchen counter — or on your knees. Build the reps over time.

Curl + overhead triceps 2 × 12 each

Curl: stand on the band, palms forward, curl the ends to your shoulders with elbows pinned to your sides, lower slowly. Triceps: pin one end under your foot or behind your hip, grip the other end and straighten that arm overhead, then lower.

Pallof press 2 × 10 / side

Knot the band into a door at chest height, to one side of you. Stand side-on, hold the end at your chest with both hands, press straight out in front and resist the pull trying to twist you. Hold a second, bring it back. Both sides. Step further away for more tension.

Tap any exercise for the how-to. Controlled reps, last 2 should feel hard, ~25–30 min. Swap for your own programme if you prefer.
